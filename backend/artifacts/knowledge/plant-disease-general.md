# Plant disease diagnosis – general guidance

## When to trust the model
- High confidence (e.g. above 85%) on a single class usually indicates a clear visual match. Still consider context: leaf age, variety, and growing conditions.
- Low confidence or similar scores for several diseases suggest overlapping symptoms (e.g. fungal leaf spots). Recommend a second image, different leaf, or expert review.

## Common confusions
- **Early blight vs Septoria leaf spot**: Both show circular spots. Early blight often has concentric rings and targets older leaves; Septoria has smaller spots with gray centers.
- **Late blight vs other foliar diseases**: Late blight can spread quickly and shows greasy-looking lesions; in humid conditions look for white mold. Prioritize control if suspected.
- **Viral vs nutrient/stress**: Yellowing and curling can be viral (e.g. TYLCV) or environmental. Viral often shows uneven mottling and is spread by whiteflies.

## Escalation
- If the user reports that treatment did not help, suggest sending a new image or contacting an agronomist.
- For viral diseases (mosaic, yellow leaf curl), there is no cure; focus on removal, whitefly control, and resistant varieties for next season.
- When in doubt, recommend human expert review rather than applying strong chemicals.
